A Social Communication & Interaction Symptoms

A1

Social Communication Difficulty

Challenges in initiating and maintaining conversations, understanding social cues, and engaging in reciprocal communication.

A2

Poor Eye Contact

Avoids or has difficulty maintaining appropriate eye contact during interactions.

A3

Limited Facial Expression

Reduced range or intensity of facial expressions during social interactions.

A4

Repetitive Behaviors

Engages in repetitive movements, speech patterns, or routines.

A5

Difficulty Expressing Emotions

Struggles to identify, express, or regulate emotions appropriately.

A6

Lack of Empathy Understanding

Difficulty understanding or relating to others' emotions and perspectives.

A7

Limited Eye Gaze Response

Does not follow or respond to others' eye gaze direction for shared attention.

A8

Unresponsive to Name

Does not consistently respond when name is called, despite normal hearing.

A9

Obsessive Interest in Specific Topics

Intense, focused interests in narrow topics, often to the exclusion of other activities.

A10

Strong Attachment to Objects

Unusual or intense attachment to specific objects, often non-toy items.

A11

Difficulty Adapting to Social Rules

Struggles to understand or follow unwritten social norms and conventions.

A12

Unusual Voice Tone

Atypical pitch, rhythm, or volume of speech (e.g., monotone, robotic, or sing-song).

A13

Poor Peer Relationships

Difficulty forming and maintaining age-appropriate friendships and peer connections.

B Additional Behavioral & Developmental Symptoms

A14

Resistance to Change

Extreme distress or difficulty when routines, schedules, or environments change.

A15

Sensory Sensitivity

Heightened or reduced sensitivity to sounds, lights, textures, tastes, or smells.

A16

Speech Delay

Late development of spoken language or difficulty with verbal communication.

A17

Echolalia

Repeats words or phrases heard from others, sometimes out of context.

A18

Unusual Eating or Sleeping Habits

Extreme food selectivity, ritualistic eating behaviors, or irregular sleep patterns.

A19

Difficulty in Group Interaction

Struggles to participate or engage appropriately in group settings.

A20

Narrow Interests

Limited range of interests, often focused on specific topics or activities.

A21

Lack of Imitation Skills

Difficulty mimicking actions, gestures, or sounds of others.

A22

Difficulty Making Eye or Physical Contact

Avoids or has difficulty initiating/maintaining eye contact and physical touch.
